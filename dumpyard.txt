                <h2>The Cultural Mosaic of Nepal</h2>
                <p>Nepal is a land of extraordinary cultural diversity, home to over 125 distinct ethnic groups, each with their own unique traditions, languages, and customs. From the Himalayan heights to the southern plains, the country's rich cultural tapestry has been woven over thousands of years, influenced by both Hindu and Buddhist traditions as well as indigenous practices.</p>
                <p>As you explore Nepal, you'll encounter a living heritage that continues to thrive in daily life, festivals, art, music, and spiritual practices. This page offers a glimpse into the cultural wonders that make Nepal truly special.</p>

                        <p>Nepal is where Hinduism and Buddhism have coexisted harmoniously for centuries. Many sacred sites are revered by both religions, and Nepalis often participate in each other's ceremonies regardless of their personal faith.</p>

                        <p>The Kumari tradition represents one of Nepal's most unique cultural practices. Young girls selected as living embodiments of divine female energy are worshipped until they reach puberty, acting as a living link between the heavenly and earthly realms.</p>
                    </div>

                        <p>From the Sherpas of the high Himalayas to the Tharus of the Terai plains, Nepal's ethnic groups maintain distinct identities through language, dress, architecture, and social customs while contributing to a unified national identity.</p>
                    </div>

                <h2>Daily Life & Social Customs</h2>
                <div class="dual-content">
                    <div class="text-content">
                        <h3>Greeting Customs</h3>
                        <p>The traditional Nepali greeting is "Namaste" (or "Namaskar" for more formal occasions), performed by pressing palms together in front of the chest with a slight bow. This greeting acknowledges the divine in each person.</p>
                        
                        <h3>Family Structure</h3>
                        <p>Nepali society is traditionally family-centered, with multiple generations often living under one roof. Elders are highly respected, and important decisions are typically made as a family unit.</p>
                        
                        <h3>Hospitality</h3>
                        <p>Guests are considered akin to gods in Nepali culture. Even in the most humble homes, visitors will be offered tea and whatever food is available as a sign of respect and welcome.</p>
                    </div>

                <h2>Cultural Practices</h2>
                <div class="practice-card">
                    <div class="practice-icon">
                        <i class="fas fa-om"></i>
                    </div>
                    <h3>Morning Rituals</h3>
                    <p>Many Nepali families begin their day with puja (prayer), offering flowers, incense, and food to household deities before starting their daily activities.</p>
                </div>
                <div class="practice-card">
                    <div class="practice-icon">
                        <i class="fas fa-moon"></i>
                    </div>
                    <h3>Lunar Calendar</h3>
                    <p>The traditional Nepali calendar (Bikram Sambat) is lunar-based and approximately 56.7 years ahead of the Gregorian calendar. Most festivals and auspicious dates are determined by this calendar.</p>
                </div>
                <div class="practice-card">
                    <div class="practice-icon">
                        <i class="fas fa-hands"></i>
                    </div>
                    <h3>Caste System</h3>
                    <p>Though legally abolished, aspects of the caste system still influence social structures in Nepal. However, modern urbanization and education are gradually diminishing these traditional divisions.</p>
                </div>
                <div class="practice-card">
                    <div class="practice-icon">
                        <i class="fas fa-utensils"></i>
                    </div>
                    <h3>Food Traditions</h3>
                    <p>Food rituals are important in Nepali culture. Eating with the right hand, sharing prasad (blessed food) after ceremonies, and specific foods for different occasions are integral practices.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
        </section>
